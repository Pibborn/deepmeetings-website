Generalization in Deep Learning
Speaker:
Mattia Cerrato
One of the many open questions about Deep Networks asks how it is possible for them to generalize properly even in the face of their number of parameters. In fact, classical learning theory attributes generalization ability to the use of a low-capacity model. A good summary of the current academic discussion might be found in [1]; moreover, the same paper discusses how high-capacity models (such as but not limited to deep nets) might have arbirarily small test errors.
